Weekly vehicle loan Payments Vs. Monthly

If you simply enter the terms for a new car loan into the weekly vehicle loan payment calculator, you will note that you may only save a couple of hundred dollars by choosing to repay the car loan using weekly vehicle loan payments vs. monthly vehicle loan payments. This is because the annual total of your weekly vehicle loan payments will be close to the same as the annual total of your monthly payments. In order to save a large chunk of time and interest, you need to increase the annual total of your payments. One way to do that -- while hardly noticing the increase -- is to simply make 1/2 of your monthly car payment every two weeks. This will result in making 26 bi-weekly payments per year, which is the equivalent of making 13 monthly payments per year instead of 12. Now you would think that we could double the savings if we use the same process to switch from monthly payments to making weekly vehicle loan payments. However, if you divide your monthly payment by 4 and pay that result on a weekly basis, you will see that the annual total of your 52 weekly payments would be identical to making 26 bi-weekly payments. Therefore, in order to significantly increase your bi-weekly vehicle loan interest savings when switching to a weekly repayment method, you would need to look at making the equivalent of 14 monthly payments per year instead of the 13 you would make using the bi-weekly payment method. In other words, your weekly vehicle loan payments would be calculated as follows:

Weekly payment = Monthly payment x 14 ÷ 52

The above formula is the one used by the weekly vehicle loan payment calculator on this page, which results in making the equivalent of 14 monthly payments per year.

Beware of Gluttonous Vehicle Finance Companies

Since switching to weekly payments results in the prepayment of principal ahead of the original car loan terms, there are numerous, predatory vehicle loan companies ready to deny your attempts to save money on interest while cutting your loan term. While some of these companies will simply escrow your prepayment and apply payments as they were scheduled (giving you "the finger" for your early payoff attempt), the more dastardly among them will go so far as to charge you a penalty for prepaying the principal. In any case, before you start making weekly vehicle loan payments, be sure to check with your car loan finance company to make sure your principal prepayments will be applied to the balance as you make them -- free of any prepayment penalties.
